Analysis of an Ethical Dilemma

A few years ago, a technician misused elevated privileges by granting unauthorized access to another users information. This action exposed sensitive, work-related files that included personally identifiable information (PII) and violated policy boundaries. The technician had no valid reason to access the device, as it was outside their assigned responsibilities.

The organization responded swiftly by suspending the technician's access, conducting a full audit of privileged accounts, and reinforcing acceptable use and access policies. The individual ultimately resigned during the investigation process.

This experience reinforced several key lessons: privileged access must be guarded with integrity; transparency and accountability are essential to ethical IT practice; and leaders must create environments where ethical boundaries are respected. It reaffirmed my own commitment to consent-based support, responsible stewardship of access, and professionalism in elevated roles.
