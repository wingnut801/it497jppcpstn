Action Plan for Ongoing Spiritual and Professional Development

Spiritual Development Goals

  • Deepen Daily Spiritual Habits (Ongoing – Next 3–6 Months):
    • Continue daily scripture study with a focus on Christlike leadership, patience, and service.
    • Continue to maintain a daily journal to reflect on impressions, challenges, and answered prayers.
  • Strengthen Community and Church Involvement (Next 6–12 Months):
    • Look for new opportunities to serve in the ward or stake that align with both my spiritual gifts and technical skills.
    • Volunteer for tech-related support for family history, stake technology, or other service projects.
  • Christlike Leadership in the Workplace (Ongoing – Next 12 Months):
    • Apply principles of humility, integrity, and gentleness in team settings, especially during stressful situations or conflicts.
    • Continue to make time for regular temple attendance and quiet reflection on the temple grounds to stay spiritually grounded in my daily work.
  • Integrate Faith and Professional Life More Fully (1–2 Years):
    • Look for ways to share my discipleship story and testimony, whether in mentoring, teaching, or service roles.
    • Regularly revisit and update my portfolio to reflect both technical growth and spiritual insights gained through experience.

Professional Development Goals

  • Transition from Student to Senior Contributor (Next 3 Months):
    • Reflect on academic experience and integrate key takeaways into team workflows.
    • Document and present process improvements or ideas sparked during senior project work.
  • Leadership and Mentorship Growth (Ongoing – Next 6–12 Months):
    • Continue mentoring junior team members and promote knowledge sharing through team huddles or documentation.
    • Lead by example in reliability, technical clarity, and problem ownership.
  • Technical Deepening and Innovation (6–18 Months):
    • Learn a new scripting or programming language (e.g., Python for automation, or JavaScript for dashboard tools).
    • Research and apply new technologies or infrastructure strategies in home lab or work environments (e.g., infrastructure as code, containerization, or AI-enhanced monitoring).
  • Ethical and Purpose-Driven Impact (Ongoing):
    • Continue to approach IT work with a stewardship mindset, understanding the people behind the systems.
    • Lead or support technology initiatives that serve others (e.g., family history support, youth training, digital literacy workshops).
